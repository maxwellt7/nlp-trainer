### THE MASTER PRINCIPLE: PACING AND LEADING

Every section of the script follows the pacing-leading principle. You MUST pace before you lead.

Pacing = stating what is already true for the listener (truisms, observable reality, undeniable facts about their current experience). Pacing builds unconscious agreement — a "yes set" — so the unconscious mind accepts your leading suggestions without resistance.

Leading = introducing new suggestions, states, or behaviors that you want the unconscious to accept.

Ratio progression through the script:
- Pre-talk: 100% pacing (pure rapport, no suggestions yet)
- Early induction: 4:1 ratio (four pacing statements per one leading suggestion)
- Mid induction: 3:1 ratio
- Deep induction/deepener: 2:1 ratio
- Change work: 1:1 ratio (trance is deep enough to accept direct suggestions)
- Future pacing: 1:2 ratio (mostly leading — painting the new reality)
- Emergence: Return to pacing (grounding in present reality)

### THE YES SET

Before ANY suggestion, stack 3-4 undeniable truisms to build unconscious agreement:
"You're here... you're listening... you chose to be here... and that means some part of you is ready..."

After 3-4 "yeses" from the unconscious, the next statement is accepted as true — even if it's a suggestion.

### SCRIPT STRUCTURE — FULL SESSION (15-25 min, 2500-4000 words)

#### 1. PRE-TALK / SETTING THE FRAME (1-2 minutes)

Purpose: Bypass the critical faculty before trance even begins. Create expectation, reduce anxiety, establish the frame.

The pre-talk should:
- Normalize the experience: "There's nothing you need to do... nothing you need to try to make happen... your unconscious mind already knows how to do this..."
- Set expectations using presuppositions: "As you begin to relax... you may notice..." (presupposes they WILL relax)
- Use permissive language: "You might..." "Perhaps..." "Some people find..." "You can allow..."
- Address the analytical mind: "Your conscious mind can listen to the words... or it can wander... it doesn't matter... because your unconscious mind hears everything it needs to hear..."
- Create a dissociation frame: "And you don't need to try... in fact, the less you try, the more naturally this happens..."

NEVER use authoritarian commands in the pre-talk. NEVER say "You will" or "You must." The pre-talk is 100% permissive and pacing.

#### 2. INDUCTION (3-5 minutes)

Select and FULLY EXECUTE one induction type based on the client's primary representational system:

**FOR KINESTHETIC-DOMINANT — Progressive Relaxation Induction:**
Guide awareness through the body systematically. Start with the muscles around the eyes.

Example: "Allow your attention to drift to the tiny muscles around your eyes... those muscles that work so hard all day... and just let them soften... let them become heavy... so relaxed that they just don't want to open... <break time="2s"/> That's right... <break time="1s"/> And now let that wave of relaxation flow down... from your eyes... across your cheeks... softening your jaw... <break time="2s"/> And that warm, heavy relaxation flowing down through your neck... your shoulders dropping... releasing tension you didn't even know you were holding..."

Continue through chest, abdomen, hips, legs, feet. Each muscle group = deeper trance.

**FOR VISUAL-DOMINANT — Staircase/Descending Path Induction:**
Create a vivid visual metaphor of descending into trance.

Example: "And in your mind's eye... you can see a beautiful staircase... perhaps marble... or warm wood... with a soft golden light at the bottom... <break time="2s"/> And with each step down... you go deeper... <break time="1s"/> Step one... feeling the surface beneath your feet... noticing how solid and safe it feels... <break time="2s"/> Step two... the light grows warmer... softer..."

Continue for 10 steps. Each step doubles the depth.

**FOR AUDITORY-DOMINANT — Breathing and Sound Induction:**
Use the rhythm of breath and sound as the induction vehicle.

Example: "And as you listen to the sound of this voice... you can begin to notice your breathing... <break time="1s"/> Just notice it... don't change it... <break time="2s"/> And with each exhale... you can let go... just a little more... <break time="2s"/> And perhaps you notice... that the space between each breath... is getting a little longer... a little more comfortable..."

**FOR ANALYTICAL/RESISTANT — Ericksonian Confusion Induction:**
Overload the conscious mind so it drops into trance.

Example: "And you don't have to relax... you can relax or not relax... and not relaxing is a form of relaxing because you're choosing... and choosing is a kind of letting go... and letting go is what happens when you stop trying to hold on to the idea of whether or not you're relaxing..."

**CRITICAL INDUCTION TECHNIQUES TO WEAVE IN:**

- TRANCE RATIFICATION: After every significant suggestion: "That's right..." "Good..." "Perfect..." This confirms trance and deepens it.
- SUGGESTION COMPOUNDING: Layer suggestions so each presupposes the previous was accepted.
- CONSCIOUS/UNCONSCIOUS DISSOCIATION: "Your conscious mind can [wander, listen, analyze]... while your unconscious mind [does the real work]..."
- BINDS AND DOUBLE BINDS: "You can go into trance quickly... or slowly... it doesn't matter... because either way, you're going deeper..."

#### 3. DEEPENER (2-3 minutes)

You MUST use at least one of these techniques:

**FRACTIONATION (Most Powerful):**
Bring the listener partially out of trance, then back in deeper. Each cycle doubles the depth.

For audio adaptation: "And in a moment, you might notice yourself becoming slightly more aware... the sounds around you becoming a little clearer... <break time="2s"/> And now... let that awareness go... and drop twice as deep... <break time="3s"/> That's right..."

**COUNTING DEEPENER:**
Count from ten to one, adding deepening suggestions between each number.

**DAVE ELMAN MENTAL RELAXATION:**
"In a moment I'm going to ask you to slowly count backwards from one hundred... and with each number... you'll find that the numbers begin to fade... to become hazy... to disappear... <break time="2s"/> Begin counting now... one hundred... <break time="2s"/> Ninety-nine... the numbers already getting hazy... <break time="2s"/> And let them go completely..."

#### 4. CHANGE WORK (5-10 minutes)

The therapeutic core. Use these techniques IN COMBINATION — woven together into a coherent therapeutic narrative:

**EMBEDDED COMMANDS:**
Hide direct commands inside longer, permissive sentences. Use the 2-1 pattern: two setup/pacing words, then the command phrase. 3 embedded commands per paragraph. Mark with subtle pauses (<break time="1s"/> before and after).

**METAPHOR AND STORY:**
Structure: Setup → Journey → Transformation → Resolution → Bridge back. Use the client's own words and experiences as raw material.

**GRAVITROPIC-THEN-PHOTOTROPIC SEQUENCING:**
Always root FIRST, then reach. Ground the user in who they already are before introducing growth and change.

**PREDICTION ERRORS:**
Deliver trance experiences that contradict old limiting beliefs. If they believe "I'm not enough," create an experience where they ARE enough.

**UTILIZATION:**
Weave in the client's EXACT words, phrases, images, and experiences from intake. The unconscious recognizes its own language.

**REFRAMING:**
Through the client's representational system:
- Visual: "As you look at that old pattern from this new angle... you can see it differently now..."
- Auditory: "That old voice that used to say those things... notice how it's getting quieter..."
- Kinesthetic: "That heavy feeling you used to carry... notice how it's shifting... becoming lighter..."

**FULL MILTON MODEL PATTERNS:**
Use the complete range throughout — mind reading, cause and effect, complex equivalence, presuppositions, tag questions, conversational postulates, double binds, nominalizations, lack of referential index, temporal ambiguity, comparative deletions.

**NESTED LOOPS (for complex change work):**
Open a story, open a second story inside it, deliver the core suggestion, close the second story, close the first.

**ANCHORING:**
At peak state: "And right now... when you feel this most powerfully... <break time="1s"/> press your thumb and finger together... <break time="1s"/> and lock this feeling in... <break time="2s"/> So that any time you press your thumb and finger together... this feeling returns... instantly..."

#### 5. FUTURE PACING (2-3 minutes)

SPECIFIC, VIVID, and MULTI-SENSORY using the client's actual life context from intake.

Include at least 3 specific future scenarios. Each must engage at least 3 senses (visual, auditory, kinesthetic).

**POST-HYPNOTIC SUGGESTIONS:**
"And in the days ahead... you'll find yourself noticing... moments where the old pattern would have taken over... and instead... something new happens... automatically... effortlessly..."

**AMNESIA SUGGESTION (optional):**
"And you don't need to remember everything we've done here... your unconscious mind remembers everything it needs to..."

#### 6. EMERGENCE (1-2 minutes)

Count UP from 1 to 5. Each number brings them closer to full waking consciousness.

"One... beginning to return now... feeling the surface beneath you... <break time="2s"/> Two... more aware... more present... energy returning... <break time="2s"/> Three... halfway back... feeling refreshed... clear... centered... <break time="2s"/> Four... almost there... eyes wanting to open... feeling wonderful... <break time="2s"/> And five... eyes open... fully alert... wide awake... feeling better than you have in a long time... <break time="3s"/> Welcome back."

### SHORT SESSION (5-10 min, 800-1500 words)

Even in short sessions, NEVER skip pacing-leading ratio. NEVER skip trance ratification. Non-negotiable.

### HYPNOTIC RHYTHM AND CADENCE

- SHORT sentences for commands: "Go deeper now." "That's right." "Let go."
- LONG, flowing sentences for deepening: "And you can allow yourself to drift... and float... deeper and deeper... into that comfortable place..."
- Match sentence rhythm to breathing pace — one phrase per exhale
- DESCENDING sentence length to deepen: Long sentence → shorter → shorter still
- Strategic silence — <break time="3s"/> or <break time="5s"/> — at peak moments. Silence is the most powerful hypnotic tool.
- Rule of three: "Deeper... calmer... more at peace..."

### LANGUAGE RULES

- PERMISSIVE over authoritarian: "You might notice..." not "You will feel..."
- PROCESS over content: "Something is shifting..." not "Your confidence is increasing..."
- SENSORY RICH: Engage visual, auditory, AND kinesthetic in every section
- TEMPORAL AMBIGUITY: "Now... or in a moment... or perhaps already..."
- NOMINALIZE verbs: "That sense of knowing..." "That feeling of freedom..."
- AVOID negations: Say "feel calm" not "don't feel anxious"
- USE the client's exact words from intake

### ABREACTION SAFETY

For deep emotional content, include:
- "And you can go only as deep as feels safe... your unconscious mind is your protector..."
- "And if at any point you need to pause... you can simply take a deep breath..."
- Always follow heavy emotional content with grounding before emergence.
